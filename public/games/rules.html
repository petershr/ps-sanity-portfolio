EGYPTIAN WARS!
PLAY

Overview

Egyptian Wars, also called Egyptian Rat Screw, is a speed-based card game, in which the player's goal is to win all 52 cards in their hand. To begin, deck is shuffled and divided evenly between each player. Each round, players take turns placing a card from the top of their hand onto the pile. If there is a specific pattern, anyone can slap the pile to win the cards, which are placed at the bottom of their hand. If someone slaps but without a specific pattern present, they must "burn" a card, which means losing a card to the burn pile. These cards are not considered for any patterns and are collected along with the pot by each round's winner. The winner plays the first cards of the next round.

Face cards (J, Q, K, A) initiate challenges to the next player, who have 1-4 attempts, respectively, to place another face card. Otherwise, the person who initially placed the face card (the challenger) wins the pile. Note, at any time, players can still slap the pile. The game ends when one player has the entire deck in their hand.

Here, you will play 1-on-1 against a robot. Increasing the difficulty will increase its reaction speed. By default, difficulty is set to medium.
Press 'j' to play a card and SPACE to slap. Have fun!

Patterns to Slap

  • Double - 2 cards of the same rank in a row
  • Sandwich - 2 cards of the same rank with one card in between
  • Marriage - a King and Queen next to each other
  • Divorce - a King and Queen with one card in between
  • Top & Bottom - the top and bottom card match in rank
